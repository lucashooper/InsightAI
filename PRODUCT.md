# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People using guided journaling to understand their emotions, notice recurring patterns, and build healthier routines. They often open Insight during quiet, personal moments and need the interface to feel safe, legible, and immediately trustworthy rather than clinical or performance-driven.

## Product Purpose

Insight turns journal entries and check-ins into useful emotional context without making reflection feel like analytics work. Success means users feel comfortable recording honestly, can understand their current emotional landscape at a glance, and return because the product feels calm and personally meaningful.

## Brand Personality

Calm, refined, luminous. The product should feel emotionally safe and intimate, with premium craft expressed through controlled light, depth, and typography rather than decorative excess.

## Anti-references

- Saturated fitness-dashboard color blocks or high-energy gamification.
- Flat, generic wellness cards with equal visual weight everywhere.
- Decorative glassmorphism applied uniformly to every surface.
- Heavy neon, glossy casino effects, excessive gradients, or ornamental motion.
- Low-contrast pale text and visual treatments that compromise legibility.

## Design Principles

1. Use light to establish hierarchy: the most meaningful insight can glow; supporting information stays quiet.
2. Make reflection feel spacious and safe, never dense or performance-coded.
3. Keep one coherent material language across light and dark themes while tuning luminosity to each environment.
4. Prefer restrained, tactile depth over extra decoration.
5. Preserve familiar interactions so visual craft never slows the journaling task.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA text contrast, retain clear non-color state cues, and support reduced-motion preferences. Animated ambient elements should be slow, subtle, and nonessential; core content and interactions must remain usable without motion.
